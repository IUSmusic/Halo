One-hand sculpt mode uses pinch to grab the ghost keyboard, move it, rotate with wrist angle, and resize using pinch opening. Rectangle mode remains available when you want explicit tracing. Voice letters/actions and word recognition are optional and can be turned on only when helpful.

How to use

  1. Start the camera and choose your camera source.
  2. Pick One-hand sculpt to place the keyboard with one hand, or Rectangle trace to trace it manually.
  3. In sculpt mode, pinch to grab the keyboard, move your hand to place it, open or close the pinch slightly to resize it, and rotate with your wrist.
  4. Press Pin keyboard when the placement feels right.
  5. Type with any fingertips. In Press gesture mode, a short poke motion triggers the key instead of simple hover timing.
  6. Optionally turn on voice letters/actions for commands like “type B”, “press enter”, “backspace”, or NATO names like “bravo”. Turn on word recognition if you also want normal spoken words typed.

Natural view now keeps overlay text readable and corrects left-right for front cameras more reliably. Press typing also uses sticky key targeting and a press-and-release gesture so it is easier to land the right key.
